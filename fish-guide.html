Atlantic Species Guide

Jupiter's waters are home to some of the most sought-after game fish in the world. Learn about the species you'll target, when to fish for them, and the techniques that work best. Our captains are experts in all these species and will put you on the fish of your dreams.

Atlantic Sailfish

Offshore

The crown jewel of Florida sportfishing. Known for their incredible jumping ability and speed (up to 68mph), Sailfish are the ultimate trophy. These acrobatic fighters put on an unforgettable show when hooked. Jupiter is considered one of the top Sailfish destinations in the world, with multiple fish days being common during peak season.

Peak Season Nov - May
Min. Size 63" (LJL)
Best Technique Live Bait, Kite Fishing
Avg. Weight 40-60 lbs

Conservation Note: We practice catch & release for all Sailfish to preserve this incredible fishery.

Mahi-Mahi (Dolphin)

Offshore

Arguably the most beautiful fish in the ocean with their brilliant gold, green, and blue colors. Mahi are aggressive feeders and excellent table fare. They often travel in schools, so multiple hookups are common. These fast-growing fish provide non-stop action and make for an exciting day on the water.

Peak Season Apr - Aug
Min. Size 20" Fork Length
Best Technique Trolling, Casting
Avg. Weight 10-30 lbs

Table Fare: Excellent eating! We'll clean and bag your catch for the grill.

Snook

Inshore

Florida's signature inshore species. Snook are powerful fighters that love structure‚Äîdocks, mangroves, and bridges. They're known for their explosive strikes and hard runs. Highly prized by anglers for both their fighting ability and delicious white meat. Snook fishing requires skill and finesse, making them a favorite challenge.

Peak Season Year Round
Slot Limit 28-33" Total
Best Technique Live Bait, Plugs
Avg. Weight 5-15 lbs

Seasonal Closure: Check current regulations as Snook have seasonal closures for spawning.

Tarpon

Inshore

The "Silver King" - one of the most spectacular game fish in the world. Tarpon are famous for their aerial acrobatics and brute strength. These prehistoric fish can grow over 200 pounds and provide battles that can last hours. Night fishing for Tarpon under the bridges is a bucket-list experience that every angler should try.

Peak Season Apr - Sep
Permit Required Tarpon Tag ($50)
Best Technique Live Crab, Mullet
Avg. Weight 80-150 lbs

Catch & Release Only: Tarpon must be released. Quick photos at boatside only.

Wahoo

Offshore

The fastest fish in the ocean, capable of speeds over 60 mph. Wahoo are prized for their blistering runs and excellent eating quality. These torpedo-shaped predators have razor-sharp teeth and strike with incredible force. Winter months bring trophy-sized Wahoo to Jupiter's offshore waters, with fish over 100 pounds possible.

Peak Season Nov - Mar
No Size Limit 2 Per Person
Best Technique High-Speed Trolling
Avg. Weight 30-80 lbs

Premium Sashimi: Wahoo is considered one of the finest eating fish in the ocean.

Red Drum (Redfish)

Inshore

A hard-fighting favorite in the flats and mangroves. Redfish are known for their copper-bronze color and distinctive black spot near the tail. They provide an incredible fight on light tackle and are available year-round. Sight fishing for "tailing" reds in shallow water is one of the most exciting forms of inshore angling.

Peak Season Year Round
Slot Limit 18-27" Total
Best Technique Cut Bait, Jigs
Avg. Weight 5-12 lbs

Great for Beginners: Redfish are aggressive feeders and perfect for first-time anglers.

Our Conservation Commitment

At OceanBound, we're passionate about preserving Florida's incredible fishery for future generations. We practice responsible catch and release techniques, use circle hooks to minimize injury, and educate our guests on proper fish handling.

All our captains are certified in proper release techniques and follow FWC guidelines strictly. We encourage catch and release for all billfish (Sailfish, Marlin) and oversized specimens. When keeping fish for the table, we promote taking only what you'll eat and staying well within legal limits.

Together, we can ensure that Jupiter's waters remain one of the world's premier fishing destinations for generations to come.
